Processing Malachite

The greenish-blue colour that you can see in most Mughal paintings is actually ground malachite. To produce this pigment, an artist would grind a piece of malachite gemstone to a fine powder. The longer the malachite is ground, the paler the colour becomes. After it is ground, the malachite powder is thoroughly washed so that no impurities can spoil the final colour.
