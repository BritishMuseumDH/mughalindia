obj4=<b>War and the seasons</b><br><br>Animals such as camels and horses made up an important part of the Mughal war machine. When we think of battles, we may think about <font color='#990000'><a href="javascript:openGlos('glossary/t/tactics.html','tact')"><u>tactics</u></a></font> weapons, and even the animals involved, but there are other factors out of the control of even the best soldiers and leaders.<br><br>Military campaigns in India depended on good weather and suitable <font color='#990000'><a href="javascript:openGlos('glossary/t/terrain.html','terr')"><u>terrain</u></a></font>. Before men could go to war at all, the harvest had to be brought in. War was governed by the seasons. Too much rain and rivers could burst their banks, leading to foot soldiers, elephants and horses being bogged down in mud.<br><br>Harsh or mountainous landscapes made getting hold of supplies very difficult. One example would be <font color='#990000'><a href="javascript:openGlos('glossary/a/aurangzeb.html','aura')"><u>Aurangzeb's</u></a></font> attempt to regain the Mughals' ancient homeland of <font color='#990000'><a href="javascript:openGlos('glossary/f/ferghana.html','ferg')"><u>Ferghana</u></a></font> from the <font color='#990000'><a href="javascript:openGlos('glossary/u/uzbeks.html','uzbe')"><u>Uzbeks</u></a></font> who were their oldest enemies. Pushing north, Aurangzeb's armies ran low on supplies. As they retreated in 1647, they found themselves caught in the snowbound <font color='#990000'><a href="javascript:openGlos('glossary/h/hindukush.html','hkush')"><u>Hindu Kush</u></a></font> mountains and many soldiers died.
