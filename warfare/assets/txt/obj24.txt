obj24=<b>Guns and seige warfare</b><br><br>This wall gun was used for more heavy duty warfare, particularly in <font color='#990000'><a href="javascript:openGlos('glossary/s/siege.html','sieg')"><u>siege</u></a></font> situations.<br><br>In a siege the Mughals had several <font color='#990000'><a href="javascript:openGlos('glossary/t/tactics.html','tact')"><u>tactics</u></a></font> up their sleeves. Akbar for example bombarded the target with siege guns which were dragged into place by elephants and bullocks. He also dug tunnels under the walls and filled them with explosives.<br><br>However, soldiers attacking a fort under siege were still <font color='#990000'><a href="javascript:openGlos('glossary/v/vulnerable.html','vuln')"><u>vulnerable</u></a></font> to <font color='#990000'><a href="javascript:openGlos('glossary/c/counterattack.html','coun')"><u>counter-attack</u></a></font>. In response, a sabat was often constructed. A sabat was a covered passageway which led up to the walls of the stronghold was under siege. It is unclear whether it was built above ground or whether it was a wide trench.<br><br>The passage was covered with tough leather so that activities could not be seen from the stronghold. The sabat was large enough for ten men to ride a breast and deep enough for a man on an elephant to move along it.<br><br>The purpose of the sabat was to bring artillery and soldiers within range of the wall, and to protect people from enemy fire.
