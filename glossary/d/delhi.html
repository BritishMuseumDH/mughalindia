Delhi

The city of Delhi in north central India, was made the capital of the Mughal Empire in 1638.

Within the city, Shah Jahan built the famous Red Fort which contained the imperial palace.

In 1739, Delhi was the site of a fierce battle. The Persian Nadir Shah massacred the people of Delhi and took the famous Peacock Throne which originally belonged to Shah Jahan.

The capture of Delhi was one of the final blows to the much weakened Mughal Empire.
