Sikhism / Sikh

The Sikh religion was founded in northern India in around AD 1500 by Guru Nanak. His intention was to create a religion which would unite Hindus and Muslims.

Amongst other things, Sikhism rejected the worship of images, mosques and temples and the caste system. In their place, the new religion stressed the importance of a virtuous life.

Sikhism's main group of followers were made up of the Jats, the farming communities of northwest India.
