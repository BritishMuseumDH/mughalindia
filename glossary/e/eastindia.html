East India Company

The English East India Company was a large group of merchants who had recognized the vast opportunites for trade and wealth in India.

After gaining the authority of a royal charter from Queen Elizabeth I in 1600, trade in spices and other goods began in earnest. Warehouses known as factories were built around India and the Company increased in wealth, influence and power.

However, by 1783 the East India Company was effectively in the hands of the British government, as was the majority of India. The Mughal Empire was no more than a name.

The East India Company was finally abolished in 1858.
