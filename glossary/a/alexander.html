Alexander the Great

Alexander the Great lived between 356 and 323 BC. He was a very successful ruler, famous for conquering vast areas across the world. He invaded Persia and conquered Egypt, Syria and Mesopotamia. When he was in Egypt, Alexander founded Alexandria, his first and best-known city.

He was thought of as a god during his own lifetime, and became a model for rulers who came after him - as well as the main character in many fantastic legends.
